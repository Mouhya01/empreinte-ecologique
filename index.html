Definition

The ecological footprint is an indicator that measures the amount of natural resources required to support our needs and absorb our waste, particularly CO₂ emissions. It is expressed in "global hectares" or in kilograms of CO₂ equivalent per year.

Why is it important?

The ecological footprint is important because it helps us understand the impact of our consumption choices and lifestyles on the planet. By comparing our footprint to the Earth's regenerative capacities, we can determine if we are living sustainably or depleting natural resources.

How to reduce it?

  1. Reduce motorized travel → prioritize walking, cycling, or public transport.
  2. Save energy → use energy-efficient appliances, turn off lights, and insulate your home.
  3. Adopt a sustainable diet → consume less meat and more local products.
  4. Limit overconsumption → buy less but better (repair, recycle, second-hand).
